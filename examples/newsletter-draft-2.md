-------- SECTION 2: ACTIONABLE TIP --------

The 3-question check every rep should run after every call

Your reps don't need a fancy scorecard. They need three questions answered in writing before they forget the call. If they can't answer, they have a gap to close.

What: End every discovery call with a written 3-question check before you move to the next task
Why: Memory degrades within 90 minutes. The signals are sharpest right after the call. If you don't capture them now, you lose them forever
How:
1. Did this call move the deal forward or backward? One sentence with a specific reason (not "good vibes")
2. What is the single most important thing I didn't learn? Name the SPICED letter (Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Engagement, Decision) that's weakest
3. What did I hear that should worry me? One signal. Silence counts as a signal. If nothing, write "nothing" (and now you have evidence)

Require this in your CRM. Make it a template field. If reps skip it, you'll see the calls they skipped correlate with the deals they lose.

-------- SECTION 4: DEEP DIVE --------

Why you're losing deals you don't even know you're losing

Most sales organizations operate on a 3-call review ceiling.

The manager picks 3 calls a month. Usually the ones where the rep flagged "wanted feedback" or the ones the manager sat in on live. The other 97% of calls happen, get transcribed by Fireflies or Gong or Sybill, and never get examined by another human. Ever.

This is the actual state of sales intelligence at most B2B companies in 2026. Gigabytes of recorded data. 3% reviewed. 97% invisible.

Here's what gets lost in the 97%.

The deal-killing signals that appear 2 to 3 calls before the deal dies

Most deals don't die on the last call. They die 3 calls earlier, in a 40-second window nobody flagged. Here are the specific patterns we see over and over:

1/ VOCABULARY REGRESSION

Your champion used to say "when we implement" and now says "if we go forward." That shift usually appears 2 calls before a deal stalls. It signals internal friction the rep hasn't surfaced yet.

"I'll bring this up but..." replacing "we're doing this" is the same signal. The champion is pre-hedging their internal pitch because they already know it won't land clean.

2/ CHAMPION EROSION

The champion in your last call asked 7 questions. In this call they asked 2. Their talk time dropped 40%. They're going quiet because something changed internally and they haven't told you yet.

The subtler version: they start saying "my team" instead of "we." Distancing language. They're already positioning for the outcome they expect.

3/ STAKEHOLDER ADDS THAT AREN'T MOVES

A new person shows up in call 4. Head of IT Security. Nobody mentioned them in calls 1 through 3. Your rep thinks "great, another stakeholder." Usually it means procurement already started with your competitor and now they're checking the boxes on you.

If the new stakeholder is from a BLOCKING function (security, legal, finance) and the champion didn't introduce them personally, it's almost always bad news.

4/ PROCESS OPACITY

"We'll figure that out internally" replacing a previously-stated decision process. In call 2 they said "we'll pick a vendor by end of April." In call 4 they say "we'll work through this on our side." That is the sound of the deal moving out of your view.

5/ RESPONSE LATENCY DOUBLING

Your champion replied in 4 hours for the first 3 weeks. Now they take 2 days. That is a 12x slowdown. It is signal, not noise. Either priorities shifted or the deal did.

What 97% invisibility actually costs

Pick a rep on your team who runs ~15 discovery calls a month.

If 1 out of every 20 calls contains a deal-killer signal the rep missed, that's 9 deals per year where the rep had information that could have saved the deal if someone else had seen it.

At a K average ACV, that's 0K per rep per year in preventable losses. Across a 10-person sales team that's M in deals you lost to signals you already had in your own data.

This is not a sales problem. This is an information retrieval problem. You already paid for the data. You're just not using it.

What to do about it (3 action steps your team can run this week)

STEP 1: Pick one rep. Pull their last 10 transcripts.

Do not try to boil the ocean. One rep, one week of calls. Roughly 10 discovery calls. Read every transcript with the SPICED framework in mind.

You will almost certainly find 2 to 3 deal-killer signals the rep missed. You will almost certainly find 1 deal that is dying that nobody has flagged.

This exercise will cost you 90 minutes. It will produce a list of specific coaching moments tied to specific quotes. Do this once and your entire view of the team's performance will shift.

STEP 2: Require the 3-question check on every call

From Section 2 above. Make it a CRM field. Non-negotiable. Reps fill it out or the deal doesn't advance in your pipeline.

This is not bureaucracy. It is a forcing function for the rep to DO the review they would otherwise skip. The answer to "what did I hear that should worry me" is where the real intel lives.

STEP 3: Run the analysis systematically instead of sampling

Once you've done Step 1 by hand and seen what's in your data, you'll understand why 3-calls-a-month manager review isn't working.

You have three real options:

> Hire a revenue operations analyst to review every call (expensive, slow, they'll miss things too)
> Buy a commercial call analysis tool (most of them produce summary slop that doesn't change outcomes)
> Build or adopt a systematic scoring framework that runs on every transcript automatically

We built option 3 for ourselves at RevGrowth. Open-sourced it this week at github.com/Revgrowth1/spiced-ai. Four modes: post-call analysis, rep coaching, pre-call prep, red-flag detection. Calculable close probability, not vibes. MIT licensed. Clone and go.

It works because the framework is tight, not because the AI is special. You could run the same system with a senior rep and a spreadsheet. The AI just makes it fast enough to do on every call instead of 3 a month.
